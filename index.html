Fallen Love Clothing Line Photo Shoot

We shot this series in a raw, urban setting to capture the emotion and grit behind the Fallen Love EP. The hoodie blends comfort with edge — just like the project. Each shot tells a story of heartbreak, healing, and growth. Dim lighting, cracked concrete, and shadowed alley walls set the tone, while subtle reds and soft grayscale edits bring out the hoodie’s message: love leaves a mark, but it also fuels resilience. I wanted the photos to feel real. No gloss, just vibe. Just like the songs, the hoodie’s for anyone who’s fallen, felt lost, and is still pushing forward. Streetwear meets emotion — that’s Fallen Love.

Graduation

These graduation shots are more than just a cap and gown moment — they mark the end of one chapter and the quiet start of a bigger one. We shot portraits to capture the pride and growth up close, and wider landscape frames to show the journey — how far I’ve come, and how much lies ahead. Every detail in the photos reflects the grind: early mornings, late nights, self-doubt, breakthroughs. This isn’t just a celebration — it’s proof that I’m just getting started.

Lake Tahoe

Shot this series at Lake Tahoe right as the sun dipped behind the mountains — sky painted in gold, purple, and fire. The stillness of the lake, the breeze in the trees, and the way the light hit the water made everything feel bigger than me. Every frame carries a quiet kind of peace — the kind you only get after going through chaos and finding a moment to just breathe. I wasn’t posing — just existing. Letting the calm soak in. This shoot wasn’t about showing off. It was about being present. Nature, reflection, growth — all in one sunset. This is what it looks like when you find beauty after the storm.

Family Parties

We came together in the heart of Colombia to celebrate 91 beautiful years of life, love, and legacy. The house was full — of laughter, music, and stories that only family can tell. Kids running around, old salsa tracks playing, the smell of homemade food in the air… it felt like time slowed down for her. Abuela sat at the center, smiling with that quiet strength she’s always carried. Ninety-one years, and still the soul of the family. Every photo captured not just a moment, but generations — roots that run deep. It wasn’t about perfection — it was about presence. These are the memories that matter most.

Street Style Modeling

Shot deep in the shadows of a dimly lit parking garage, this series captures a raw, nostalgic energy. My subject wears baggy jeans, layered chains, and a loose flannel — a modern grunge-meets-streetwear vibe. The lighting is gritty and cinematic, casting soft shadows across concrete walls while overhead fluorescents create harsh contrast, highlighting emotion in every angle. Smoke drifts in one shot, surrounding him like a ghost of thought, while in another, he looks upward — lost in something bigger, something unspoken. The muted tones and blurred lights amplify a sense of solitude and rebellion. These aren’t just fashion shots — they’re a feeling. A moment of calm inside chaos.
